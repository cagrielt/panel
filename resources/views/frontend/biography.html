Devrim Gözüaçık Kimdir?

Koç University School of Medicine faculty member Professor Dr Devrim Gozuacik was born in 1971. He graduated from Izmir Saint-Joseph French College (1986) and Izmir Atatürk High School (1989) with honors. Graduated from Hacettepe English Faculty of Medicine (1995) with the title of medical doctor (MD). Gözüçık worked on Genetic Diseases at Hacettepe Children's Hospital (1990-1995), and on blood cancer (leukemia) at the Netherlands Erasmus University Hospital Cancer Center (1994). He received his MSc in Biochemistry from Paris Ecole Polytechnique (1997) and his PhD in Molecular Biology and Cancer Biology from Paris Pasteur Institute and Paris-Sud University (2001). He did his postdoctoral research at the Weizmann Institute of Science (2001-2006). He started his lab at Sabancı University (SU) in Turkey and became a professo  at Molecular Biology Genetics and Bioengineering Program (2006-2020). He specializes in molecular medicine research, especially in cancer, degenerative disease mechanisms and programmed cell death. He has been continuing his scientific and academic studies at Koç University since 2020.

Since 2018, he is an affiliate member of the University of New Mexico Autophagy, Inflammation and Metabolism Biomedical Research Center of Excellence in the USA. Prof. Gozuacik is internationally recognized in the fields of programmed cell death, cancer and disease biology, as well as the field of autophagy, which is an important sub-branch of medical research (See Autophagy researchers article).

Participating as an invited speaker (Keynote and Plenary Speaker) in more than 100 national and international conferences, including Gordon, Keystone and EMBO Conferences, Prof. Gozuacik has more than 90 scientific publications that have received more than 8000 citations in SCI. The h-factor is 23-27 (See ResearcherID, Scopus and Google Scholar). He has 9 international patents/patent applications (See Google Patents). Prof. Gozuacik's scientific research are supported by European Union (EU), European Molecular Biology Institution (EMBO), European Molecular Biology Laboratories (EMBL), South Korea National Research Foundation (NRF), UK Newton Foundation, NATO, French Cancer Research Institution ( It has been supported by various institutions and organizations including ARC), the French Medical Research Institute (FRM), Weizmann Science Institute, TÜBİTAK and the Turkish Academy of Sciences (TÜBA).

Prof. Gozuacik is a member of the International Advisory Board of the University of New Mexico Autophagy, Inflammation and Metabolism Center of Excellence for Biomedical Research. In addition, he is a member of the Board of Directors of the "International Cell Death Society" headquartered in New York. He is the Associate Editor of Autophagy (Impact factor: 16, Tylor & Francis Publishing), one of the most important journals in the field of Cell Biology. He is on the Editorial Board of Cell Communication and Signaling (Impact factor: 7.45, Springer Nature). prof. He is a referee for many highly cited international journals. Also, Cancer Research UK, Wellcome Trust UK (UK), The Health Research Board Ireland (Ireland), Research Foundation Flanders FWO (Belgium), University of Leuven (Belgium), Israel Science Foundation (Israel), University of South Florida (USA).), has been appointed as project referee and academic referee by many international research institutions including University of Pretoria (South Africa). Prof. Gozuacik's work has been recognized with various national and international awards. In 2006, Hoffmann-La Roche Pharmaceutical Company was selected as one of the "Leaders to Shape the Future of Bioscience". He was also deemed worthy of the European Molecular Biology Institution EMBO-SDIG Award in 2006 and the Turkish Academy of Sciences Outstanding Young Scientist Award (TÜBA-GEBİP) in 2008. His work on microRNAs and autophagy was started by Prof. Önder Öztunalı was awarded with the Science Award. In the same year, he was honored with the TGC Sedat Simavi Health Sciences Award, one of the most important awards in the field of medicine in Turkey. prof. The SUTAB medical device project, in which Gozuacik is included in the research team, was awarded the Elginkan Foundation Technology Award in 2015.

Prof. Gozuacik is making an intense effort for the advancement of scientific and technological research and studies in Turkey. Between 2016-2019, he served on the Scientific Advisory Board of the Biotechnology Institute of the Presidency of Turkish Health Institutes (TÜSEB). He served as project and academic referee for institutions and organizations such as TÜBİTAK, TÜBA, TR Ministry of Development, TR Ministry of Science, Industry and Technology, and many universities. He is a founding member of the Molecular Biology Association (MBD) and Cell Death Research Association (HÖAD) in Turkey. He is also on the Editorial Board of the Turkish Journal of Biology, one of the National journals in Turkey's SCI list. He organized 5 international conferences/symposiums in Turkey and took part in scientific committees in dozens of conferences. He is the founding member and board member of EFSUN, the first YÖK approved nano diagnostic center of excellence in Turkey (See EFSUN website).

Prof. Gozuacik also attaches great importance to open-minded education. In the last 16 years, it has trained more than 15 Master's, 10 Doctoral students and 15 Post-Doctoral Researchers. Of its former students, 5 work as university lecturers, 4 hold senior positions in pharmaceutical companies, others are pursuing doctorates or working as postdoctoral researchers. In addition, dozens of undergraduate students and interns from around the world have been actively trained in its laboratory.

Prof. Gozuacik provides theoretical and practical (laboratory) training to undergraduate and graduate students on molecular biology, microbiology, developmental and cancer biology, cell death mechanisms, molecular medicine and nanotechnology applications in medicine. Since 2011, he has been offering popular science courses titled "Molecular Biology, Gene Engineering and Nano Medicine" for high school students at the SU High School Summer School, adults at the SU Academy of Science and Culture, and companies at the SU Executive Development Unit (EDU). In addition, since 2011, he has been giving lectures on cancer, autophagy and disease molecular biology in credit courses for medical doctors, specialists and academicians. He made interviews in many newspapers and TV programs in order to keep science on the public agenda (See G Lab in Newspapers/TV).

He is fluent in English and French. He is married and has two children.
